About Me

Eugenia was born in 1994 in Rome and she graduated in Industrial Design and in Product Design at "La Sapienza" University of Rome. During her studies, she developed several skills that concretized in designing projects regarding many design fields. In the 2016 she spent six months in France for an Erasmus programme and during the 2017 in the Netherlands as well. These experiences together with the internships she had within a graphic studio, Ginko, and a famous italian company, Covo Srl, empowered her abilities and knowledge about the design discipline in the world. She took part in the Caravana Design Weijdstrid in the Netherlands as one of the ten contest finalists and she exhibited her project during the WTC Expo in the 2018. She completed the academic path with a thesis focused on the emotional memory and sensorial values of daily life objects, designing a writing desk that could well embodied these concepts. At this regard, she could have her design realized thanks to a famous and big carpentry of Rome, Antonacci Falegnamerie Srl. She is now searching for a new experience to professionally grow and deepen her passion and abilities in the field.

Philosophy

With her roots in Italy, Eugenia could have well learnt and studied the importance and the value of the industrial design in the society and in the entire world. Learning from the previous great designers and personalities of the past, mostly italian and nordic lessons, and the current actors in this huge field, following an approach that starts from the user and returns to him, Eugenia developed a project thread that focuses on the empathy. She is indeed sensible to empathic and emotional approaches that embrace tradition, materials and users. Her design philosophy takes care of the hidden values of an object, its memory, history, materiality, together with a deep analysis of those needs of the customer which are hidden and even the user may not be aware of, in order to reach an appropriate solution that transforms those values and needs into valuable products. She spent some periods abroad where she could experience different and interesting design modalities and cultures that in some way have positively contaminated her approaches and knowledge in the field and have expanded and enriched her capabilities. Her projects show the passion and the committment she puts in them and the attention and interest she focuses on the consumer and the memory, meant as an historical container emboding human senses interactions, materiality, emotions and values, of the object she is working on. An emotion-centered design that, in a world more and more digital, virtual and high tech, wants to return humans, human.
